Speakers

Marcin Stępień

Architectural Tradeoffs for Clinical Trials Data

👨‍🔬 About the speaker: Marcin Stępień is a Staff Software Engineer at Fluxon and an independent software vendor operating through smart.biz.pl since 2005. With over 20 years of experience in software engineering, he has spent more than a decade specializing in Clinical Data solutions for major pharmaceutical companies. Beyond his professional work, Marcin is a community facilitator for the Complexity Explorers Kraków (https://lnkd.in/drADjsjh ) group, engaging in analytical philosophy to explore the limits of understanding and computation. In his talk, 𝗔𝗿𝗰𝗵𝗶𝘁𝗲𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗮𝗹 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗲𝗼𝗳𝗳𝘀 𝗳𝗼𝗿 𝗖𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗶𝗰𝗮𝗹 𝗧𝗿𝗶𝗮𝗹𝘀 𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮, Marcin Stępień will examine the design tradeoffs in modern data systems, analyzing how structural choices impact a platform's ability to adapt to new therapeutic areas. The evolution of Data Pipeline Architectures, moving from ETL batches to event-driven meshes enabled real-time monitoring and analytics but introduced significant overhead in terms of observability and data validation. This progression, from MapReduce to the modern Data Lakehouse, is now being followed by a further architectural shift prompted by the adoption of Large Language Models and related technologies. For data interoperability in large data ecosystems, solutions like Ontobroker served as the central semantic middleware in clinical trials, using formal logic and inference engines to broker queries across heterogeneous databases by mapping them to a unified clinical ontology. Over the last years, its role has transitioned from a rigid, rule-based reasoning engine to an LLM-augmented broker that automates the previously manual and labor-intensive task of semantic term reconciliation and ontology alignment. The emergence of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and vector search compels architects to re-evaluate the balance between industry-standardized solutions and bespoke in-house approaches for gaining a competitive edge by delivering valuable insights.

Milena Di Vittorio

Computational Modeling of DARS: A Bioinformatic Approach to Targeted Nano-Drug Delivery

🔬 About the speaker: Milena Di Vittorio is an independent Italian researcher and biotech innovator specializing in the computational architecture of targeted drug delivery systems. Her research combines nanotechnology and bioinformatics, with molecular modeling at its core, aimed at enhancing the DARS (Drug Apatite Release System). By integrating principles of protein crystallization, DNA molecular structure analysis, and voltage-gate system simulations, she develops "patent pending" nanorobotic frameworks designed for precision medicine. ​Her technical approach involves the study of molecular docking and CD44-targeting ligands, utilizing bioinformatic insights to enhance the delivery of therapeutics in pulmonary and oncological contexts. Milena’s work has been recognized at international scientific forums, including FAST "I giovani e le scienze" in Milan and the Elhuyar Zientzia Azoka in Bilbao, where she presented her data-driven models for nanomedicine. Her research has attracted the attention of leading European clinical centers and institutes like CIC biomaGUNE. In her talk, 𝗖𝗼𝗺𝗽𝘂𝘁𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝗮𝗹 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗼𝗳 𝗗𝗔𝗥𝗦: 𝗔 𝗕𝗶𝗼𝗶𝗻𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗰 𝗔𝗽𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗮𝗰𝗵 𝘁𝗼 𝗧𝗮𝗿𝗴𝗲𝘁𝗲𝗱 𝗡𝗮𝗻𝗼-𝗗𝗿𝘂𝗴 𝗗𝗲𝗹𝗶𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘆, Milena will present the computational foundations of DARS and discuss how bioinformatic methods can support the design of targeted drug delivery systems. The presentation will explore the use of molecular docking, ligand-receptor interaction modeling, and high-throughput biological data analysis to improve the specificity of therapeutic delivery. She will place particular focus on CD44-targeting strategies, computational simulations of biological microenvironments, and the role of electrochemical modeling in predicting controlled drug release. Milena will also discuss how algorithmic approaches can be used to model interactions between nanomaterials and biological systems, helping researchers evaluate potential delivery strategies before moving to experimental testing.

👩‍🔬 Panelist: Agnieszka Konkolewska

Senior Bioinformatics Technical Service Scientist at QIAGEN Agnieszka holds a PhD in Molecular Biotechnology from Adam Mickiewicz University in Poznań, Poland, where her doctoral research focused on plant responses to trace metals, including zinc, cadmium, and lead uptake, transport, and detoxification. She later moved toward applied bioinformatics, data analytics, and computational approaches to agricultural and life science questions. In Ireland, she worked with UCD Professional Academy, Teagasc, VistaMilk Research Ireland Centre, and the Insight Research Ireland Centre for Data Analytics, contributing to research on sustainable agriculture, pasture improvement, forage quality, genomic prediction, and data-driven decision support. Her work has included connecting biological data with statistical and machine-learning methods, as well as developing and evaluating calibrations using near-infrared spectroscopy (NIRS) for practical biological and agricultural applications. Agnieszka has recently joined QIAGEN, where she continues to work at the intersection of life science, data, and technology. She is especially interested in making complex biological data more interpretable, useful, and connected to real-world scientific questions.

👨‍🔬 Panelist: Michał Burdukiewicz

Head of the Laboratory of Bioinformatics and Multi-omics Analysis at the Medical University of Bialystok and Senior Researcher at Vilniaus universitetas / Vilnius UniversityMichał Burdukiewicz is a bioinformatician working at the intersection of machine learning, multi-omics, and reproducible computational biology. His research focuses on the development and evaluation of predictive models for biological data, with particular emphasis on fair and FAIR benchmarking, model generalization, and the reliability of AI-driven decision systems in bioinformatics. His contributions include AmpGram, an n-gram-based predictor of antimicrobial peptides, and a body of work examining how biased benchmark design can distort the apparent performance of peptide prediction tools. To support more reliable benchmarking, his work also includes the development and curation of biological databases that provide well-structured and reusable datasets for model evaluation. Alongside his methodological work, he develops open and user-friendly omics software, including tools from the HaDeX ecosystem (biogenies.info) for hydrogen-deuterium exchange mass spectrometry analysis. His broader goal is to make computational biology more transparent, comparable, and reproducible.

👩‍💻 Panelist: Wiktoria Wilman

Bioinformatics Scientist ConsultantWiktoria Wilman is a Bioinformatics Scientist Consultant with 5 years of industry experience, helping clients navigate complex bioinformatics analyses. Alongside her consulting work, she lectures in Bioinformatics at Polish-Japanese Academy of Information Technology (PJATK) and is a co-author of scientific publications in machine learning applied to antibody research. She has also been recognized as one of Poland's Top 100 Women in Data Science by Perspektywy Women in Tech, a well-deserved acknowledgment of her contributions to the field. But Wiktoria's impact goes well beyond her professional work. Known online as Biokoderka, she runs one of Poland's most recognized social media channels dedicated to bioinformatics that reaches hundreds of students and demystifies topics like machine learning and career paths in computational biology. Her mission is to be the "big sister" in a field that's still finding its footing in Poland so that no talented young scientist feels alone in this space.

👩‍🔬 Panelist: Agata Kalita

EMBO Postdoctoral FellowAgata Kalita is a molecular biologist and EMBO Postdoctoral Fellow working at the intersection of comparative genomics and epigenetics, with a particular interest in the evolution of sex chromosomes and dosage compensation. She is currently a postdoctoral researcher at Jagiellonian University, after completing her PhD at the Institute of Molecular Biology (IMB), Germany and earlier working at the University of Virginia, USA. In recognition of her research, she has received an EMBO Postdoctoral Fellowship, a Boehringer Ingelheim Fonds PhD Fellowship, and the 2025 Elisabeth Gateff Prize. She came to bioinformatics as a molecular biologist, learning it out of necessity and curiosity in order to explore her own datasets in depth and make better use of published data. Her work focuses on non-model insects, adding a layer of complexity often overlooked in bioinformatics: a lot of current tools are validated on data-rich model species, and they can fail to perform when applied elsewhere.

🌌 Discussion Panel

Bioinformatics Beyond Earth: Challenges and Directions in Space Exploration

As humanity looks toward the Moon and Mars, bioinformatics is finding its place among the stars. This panel will explore how computational biology, space biotechnology, and AI-driven life sciences are shaping the future of space missions — from understanding biological responses to microgravity, to developing sustainable food systems and next-generation space medicine.

Format: 60-minute moderated discussion

👩‍🚀 Panel Host

Paweł P. Łabaj — Co-Founder & Co-CEO of onebiome / Deputy Director for Science at Małopolska Centre of Biotechnology UJ

Paweł is co-founder of Onebiome Sp. z o.o. Paweł focuses on developing advanced data-exploration methodologies and applying them to current biomedical challenges, with a strong emphasis on transcriptomics, metagenomics, and precision medicine. He plays a leading role in the SEQC consortium coordinated by the U.S. FDA, contributing to benchmarking large-scale gene-expression technologies and, in SEQC’s second phase, investigating how targeted RNA-Seq complements genomic sequencing in advanced cancer diagnostics. He is also a key member of the international MetaSUB Consortium, where he helps define analytical frameworks for environmental metagenomics to improve understanding of urban health, well-being, and forensic applications. Paweł co-develops the Gaia AI Factory consortium, contributing expertise in building digital twins of environmental microbiomes to support European AI infrastructure for public-health innovation and personalized medicine. As part of the global STOC Infection Working Group, he applies his spatiotemporal omics expertise to advance novel strategies for combating infectious diseases.

👨‍🚀 Panelist

Daniela Bezdan — Space Biotech Entrepreneur, Scientist

Daniela Bezdan is the CEO of dxOmics GmbH, an AI-driven precision genomics company integrating long-read sequencing and automated bioinformatics to deliver personalized medicine. With a background in biotechnology, she leads global initiatives at the intersection of genomics and space science as Chair and Founder of ISSOP (International Standards of Space Omics Procedures), Chair of the NASA GeneLab Microbe Working Group, ESA Topical Team and ESF panel member, and Ambassador of the XPRIZE Space Exploration Challenge. Author of 60 publications and 14 patents, she bridges science, technology, and entrepreneurship to advance genomics on Earth and in space.

👩‍🚀 Panelist

Agata Rudolf — Research Adjunct at Space Technology Centre, AGH University of Science and Technology of Krakow

Dr. Agata leads the Space Biology and Astrobiology Laboratory at the Faculty of Space Engineering, AGH University of Krakow. A board member of the Polish Astrobiological Society and a member of NASA GeneLab, she studies how spaceflight conditions affect living organisms and how to mitigate these effects. Her research focuses on cellular energetics, including metabolism, oxidative stress, and spaceflight as a model of accelerated aging, with a particular emphasis on cosmic radiation. As a co-founder of the Space Adaptation and Evolution sub-AWG within NASA GeneLab. Agata investigates evolutionary processes in space environments. She also has extensive experience in linking genetic variability to phenotype and adaptation, and is integrating transcriptomics into upcoming space-biology projects.

He's a lecturer, speaker, and expeditor of the first Analog Astronaut Training Center Arctic mission in Hornsund, and has participated as an astrobiologist in the lunar analog missions Eternity and EMMPol-1.
